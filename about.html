About the artist

The hands, heart and patience behind every petal.

“I don’t paint flowers — I grow them, petal by petal, out of clay.”

Lathika works in air-dry clay, an unforgiving medium that rewards only patience. Each rose begins as a plain lump of clay; hours later it has veined leaves, curled petals with paper-thin edges, and a blush of color laid on by hand. Nothing is molded, cast or mass-produced — if you look closely at two of her roses, no two petals repeat.

Her work gathers into three families: wall panels, where sculpted blooms climb across framed relief boards; vases and vessels, thrown forms and found silhouettes overgrown with clay flowers; and free-standing florals and figures, from single stems to full bouquets and figurative sculpture.

What began at a kitchen table has grown into a life's practice — work that has traveled into homes of collectors, friends and family across continents. Every piece that leaves her studio is one of a kind, and every one is a little garden that will never wilt.
